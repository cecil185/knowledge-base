# Not So Locked In Any More (Simon Willison)

**Source:** https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/14/not-so-locked-in/
**Read:** 2026-05-18

Simon Willison reflects on Mitchell Hashimoto's observation that "Programming languages used to be LOCK IN, and they're increasingly not so."

The piece centers on a conversation with someone at a technology company who had just completed an AI-driven rewrite of legacy iPhone and Android applications using React Native. When asked why they selected this approach, they explained that React Native had matured considerably and met all their requirements. Notably, they acknowledged they could migrate back to native development if circumstances warranted it.

This anecdote illustrates a broader shift: the decreasing strategic importance of initial technology choices. Coding agents are reducing the comparative costs of maintaining separate native applications, which changes the calculus around platform selection. The decision becomes less about permanent commitment and more about pragmatic flexibility.

The key insight is that AI-assisted coding tools — coding agents in particular — are making large-scale rewrites and migrations feasible at a fraction of the previous cost. When rewriting from one stack to another becomes cheap, the lock-in penalty of any given technology choice drops dramatically. Teams can choose the best tool for today knowing that switching costs tomorrow will be much lower than they historically were.
